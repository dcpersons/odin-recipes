PB&J

Description

Well, well, well. You think you're up to the task of creating one of America's greatest sandwiches? You can try, but there is no guarantee you will make it out alive. Remember: there's no shame in giving up.

You're still here? All right then. Lace up your boots, get out your dullest butter knife, and prepare to get sticky. Things are about to get crazy.

Ingredients

  • Any peanut butter
  • Strawberry jelly (grape is acceptable if you're extremely desperate)
  • Pre-sliced white bread (Wonder or store-brand)

Steps

  1. Place bread on plate

    Remove two slices of bread from plastic and place them on your plate. In the event of a bread heel, make sure to place it white-side up.

  2. Remove lids

    Place your jars of peanut butter and jelly near your plate and remove the caps. Most caps will be removable by twisting in a counter-clockwise motion.

    If your hands got sticky, DO NOT wash them. Until your sandwich has been fully consumed, this is a losing battle.

  3. Spread jelly

    With a butter knife in one hand, turn your jelly jar 45 degrees over one of your slices of bread. Use the knife to scrape out a glob of delicious sugary mush onto the desired slice of bread. Spread evenly across the surface of the bread.

  4. Rinse knife

    Rinse off knife to avoid cross-contaminating our peanut butter with our jelly. It is worth noting that this order of ingredients is best practice to avoid peanut allergens from contaminating your jelly so your mother-in-law with a severe peanut allergy doesn't end up in the hospital after making herself some toast while she's visiting.

  5. Spread peanut butter

    Using your knife, scoop a healthy dollop of peanut butter from the jar onto your naked slice of bread. Gently spread the peanut butter to create an even layer across the surface of the bread.

  6. Close sandwich

    Pick up the piece of bread housing the peanut butter, turn it upside-down, and place it onto the other piece of bread so the peanut butter and jelly are now kissing.

  7. (Optional) Cut sandwich

    If you are preparing this sandwich for a young child or autistic wife, they may prefer the sandwich cut into small triangles. If so, simply press your butter knife into the top of the sandwich diagonally across the surface. For the extra autistic, rotate the sandwich 180 degrees and repeat, then use the knife to remove the crust.

  8. Clean up

    Replace the lids on your peanut butter and jelly, and close the bread bag with the included fastener. Place your knife in the sink for somebody else to clean later.

  9. Serve and enjoy!
